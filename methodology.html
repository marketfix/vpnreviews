Methodology

How reviews
are built

The site favors current official material first, then uses independent comparison patterns to shape the layout and reading flow.

What matters most

Pricing is checked against current official plan pages or official deal pages, not recycled aggregator tables.

Trust claims are weighed against concrete support material such as audits, no-logs assurance work, open-source statements, or provider support documentation.

Everyday usability still matters. A VPN can have a strong privacy story and still be awkward for a normal buyer to live with.

Why the desktop layout changed

Wider review sites tend to work better when the hero stays short, summary cards use equal widths, and the page separates quick-reference material from the main reading flow. That pattern is common in hardware, software, and security review publishing because it makes long pages easier to scan without turning them into narrow text walls.

Product fit

Who the service suits best, who should avoid it, and what the practical buying case looks like.

Daily use

How the app behaves once installed, whether the controls are clear, and how much friction the product adds in normal use.

Technical notes

Protocols, device limits, free tiers, specialty routing, audits, and public verification where relevant.

Editorial standards

Current public provider pages change often, especially pricing and deal pages, so review pages include a source check and a refreshed date. The goal is not to freeze every number forever. The goal is to tell a reader what the product looks like right now and where that conclusion came from.

Affiliate links

Some outbound links may be affiliate links. That is disclosed in the footer rather than repeated inside the body copy. Affiliate relationships do not change the site’s core review structure.

What is not done

The site does not lean on random coupon websites for deal claims when official provider material says those coupons may be fake, expired, or automatically applied at checkout. It also avoids padding review pages with filler metrics that do not help a buyer make a decision.
